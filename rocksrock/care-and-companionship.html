Care & Companionship

Building Lasting Friendships with Your Pet Rock

How to Care for Your Rock

Luckily, caring for a pet rock is about as stress-free as it gets. No feeding schedules, vet visits, or potty training required. Still, we recommend a comfy resting spot (windowsills are popular), regular dusting, and occasional words of affirmation.

  • Find a comfortable resting spot (windowsills are ideal)
  • Dust gently with a soft cloth when needed
  • Provide occasional words of encouragement
  • Keep away from water if painted
  • Handle with care during moves

Bonding Ideas

Want to go above and beyond? Decorate a habitat with mini furniture or felt blankets. Give your rock a name and backstory. Take selfies together. Make them a profile on social media (yes, RockTok is real). These little gestures help build a meaningful, if very quiet, friendship.

Benefits of Pet Rock Companionship

Pet rocks are stress relievers, conversation starters, and reliable study buddies. They won't interrupt your Zoom call, but they will be there when you need a mental break. In a world of distractions and noise, sometimes what we need most is a little stillness — and a good rock.
